Actuated Experiences: Design and Fabrication for Interactivity

This course introduces undergraduate students to creative design and fabrication techniques that transform traditionally static objects into interactive ones, using mechanical, electrical, or physical/chemical methods. Each week, students will explore foundational technologies and fabrication techniques, including 3D modeling, rapid prototyping (3D printing and laser cutting), sensing and electronics, and graphical user interface development. The course includes five individual mini-projects and a group final project, with weekly milestones, instructor check-ins, and in-class reviews.
