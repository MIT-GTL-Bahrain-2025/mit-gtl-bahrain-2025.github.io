Lab 1: Introduction to Prototyping

Lab Overview

This lab introduces students to the basics of prototyping and rapid fabrication using accessible tools and techniques. Students will gain hands-on experience in designing and fabricating their first prototypes using 3D modeling and rapid prototyping technologies such as 3D printing and laser cutting.

Objectives

By the end of this lab, students will be able to:

  • Understand the fundamentals of prototyping
  • Create simple 3D models using design software
  • Use 3D printing and laser cutting machines to fabricate parts
  • Evaluate the quality of a prototype and iterate on the design

Materials and Tools

For this lab, the following materials and tools will be provided:

  • Access to 3D modeling software (e.g., Tinkercad, Fusion 360)
  • 3D printer (PLA filament)
  • Laser cutter and acrylic sheets
  • Basic prototyping tools (e.g., calipers, glue, cutters)

Procedure

  1. Begin by sketching your design on paper, ensuring it can be translated to a 3D model.
  2. Use 3D modeling software to create your design digitally.
  3. Prepare your model for 3D printing or laser cutting by exporting it in the required format (e.g., STL for 3D printing).
  4. Fabricate the prototype using the 3D printer or laser cutter as per the design requirements.
  5. Inspect the prototype for defects or improvements and make necessary adjustments to the design.
  6. Re-iterate the process if needed to improve your prototype.

Checkoff 1

Before the end of the lab, you must demonstrate your completed prototype to the instructor or TA. Make sure to explain the design, fabrication steps, and any challenges faced during the process.

Submission Guidelines

At the end of the lab, submit the following:

  • Your final 3D model file (e.g., STL format)
  • Pictures of your fabricated prototype
  • A brief report outlining the design process and any challenges encountered
