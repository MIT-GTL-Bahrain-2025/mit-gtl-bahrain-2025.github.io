Automata are mechanical devices designed to perform ‚Äúhuman/animal-like‚Äù actions. They usually serve a performative or artistic purpose and show a high level of craftsmanship and understanding of mechanism design. Karakuri („Åã„Çâ„Åè„Çä) is the Japanese term for automata, where the automata reflect stories or parts of Japanese culture like a ‚ÄúChahakobi Ningyou‚Äù which is an automata that bows as tea is being served.

In this lab, you will explore CAMs, gears, and linkages* to design automata. First, you'll learn the principles through a lecture and answer design understanding questions.

Next, in pairs, you'll design a Bahrain-themed automaton, inspired by cultural elements like Hiya Biya, Ramadan, Eid, Gergaoon, palm trees, pearl diving...etc

In this lab, you will learn how to:

  • ‚úÖ Design CAMs, Gears, and linkages* based on mechanical principles
  • ‚úÖ Design CAMs, Gears, and linkages* in Fusion360
  • ‚úÖ Create Assemblies in Fusion360 for motion animation
  • ‚úÖ Design a Bahrain-themed automata from sketch, prototype, to final product

üî® Fabrication Quest of the Day

  • Today, you will CAD a Bahrain-themed automata that you will then lasercut. Your design guidelines are:
    1. Automata housed in semi-closed box with dimensions 15 cm by 15 cm by 10 cm
    2. All your exported automata dxf design must fit in a wood panel of XXcm by XXcm
    3. Your design must have a crank for manual actuation
    4. Use two motions via two different mechanisms (e.g., CAM and gear).
    5. Design a story and pick two motions by referring to the mechanism glossry tables ( Table-1, Table-2 ).
    6. Start with a shaft and add components.
    7. Base your design on Bahraini themes like Eid, Hiya Biya, or pearl diving.
    8. Your design should hide the mechanism from view from the end that users will see it, and be able to see mecahnsim from the other end
    9. Keep it simple; complex automata take too long.
    10. Think ahead of how you want to add color; we will do this next lab but think ahead!
    11. Be creative! Sometimes CAMs can be used to make music and sound through carefully timed motions.
  • Fabricated results will be for next lab, but you will actuate your files through Fusion today, and upload your files for lasercutting to this Folder;

    Part (0): Mechanism Glossary Tables (Refer to this As you Design)


    CAMs

    # Mechanism Name Shaft Location Input Motion Output Motion Example Applications in Automata Video Examples of Built Automata
    1 Circular CAM At CAM center Rotational Rotational (only if the CAM touches the follower away from follower center) Dancing/Turning (eg, Ballerina) Dancing Birds (see middle bird)
    2 Circular Eccentric CAM Off CAM's center Rotational Motion is reciprocal linear (go up down etc) if the cam contacts the follower's center; off-center contact adds rotational motion. Reveal and Hide (eg. noodle appears disappears) CAM+ string (no follower-- gives pull and release motion)
    3 Elliptical/Egg-shaped CAM (lobe CAM) Shaft location based on desired change in height between event "periods" Rotational Motion is purely linear if the cam contacts the follower's center; off-center contact adds rotational motion. Similiar to eccentric. Example here is "Flipping motion" (rotate to one side, then rotate to other side using two CAMs)
    1. Skeleton Band
    2. Head Turning "Man"
    3. The King of England
    4. Two-faced Cat
    5. Cooker
    4 Snail CAM Mostly at center of CAM but Shaft location based on desired change in height between event "periods" Rotational (only one direction of rotation is valid, other one will jam the mechanism) Sudden Change in Motion (e.g. slow rise followed by sudden drop) Sudden motion, like diggin, breaking, stepping, eating chopping etc.
    1. Eating Breakfast
    2. Woodcutter Chopping Wood
    5 Irregular/Regular Shape CAM (heart, hexagonal, star etc) Varies beased on design Rotational (might have one direction of rotation valid and other one will jam mechanism) Varies based on design Depends on design-- star creates short up/down motion
    1. Vegetable Patch
    2. Start Video at 1:28
    You can design a CAM how you like but make sure you don't design a CAM that will jam. Think, what designs will jam reagrdless of rotational direction?

    1 Spur Gear transmits/change speed, force, direction of rotation Continuous Rotational Continuous Rotational (gear ratios affect the speed/force) two simultaneous rotations with equal or different speeds Jack in the Green
    2 Intermittent Gears transmits/change speed, force, direction of rotation Continuous Rotational Intermittent rotations (partial rotations or interrupted rotations) Flipping motion (rotate to one side, then rotate to other side)
    1. Skeleton Band

    3. The King of England
    4. Two-faced Cat

    3 Linear Gears Same as spur gear, but changes the type of motion from rotational to linear OR from linear to rotational Rotational (or linear) Linear (rotational if input is linear )

    2. Weight Carrying Clown
    4 Peg/Pin Gears Transmits motion and force like the other gears but also changes the axis of rotation (ie change from z-axis to y-axis etc) Rotational Rotational (around a new axis) Eating/Chpopping/Stepping
