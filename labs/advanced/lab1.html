Lab 1: ADVANCED!

Introduction

Laser cutting is a manufacturing process that uses a laser to cut materials into precise shapes and designs. In this lab, we aim to create a dynamic calendar using laser cutting. Through this lab, you will learn how to:

  • Use CAD tools like Fusion360
  • Laser-cut materials
  • Post-process your cut material
  • Assemble the final product

Before we start, please make sure you understand the following safety precautions:

  • Materials compatibility (thickness and material type)
  • Fumes and proper ventilation
  • Eye protection and proper laser cutter use
  • Always consult the shop manager in D-Lab before laser cutting!

Materials

In this lab, we will use the following equipment and materials:

  • Laser cutting machine
  • Fusion360 (CAD software)
  • Wood for the calendar
  • Prefabricated pieces (x3)
  • Wood glue
  • Safety goggles
  • Protective gloves
  • Sandpaper
  • Spray paint
  • Masking tape

Procedure

Part 0: Understanding Fusion360 Workspace (3 mins)

Watch the introductory video on the Fusion360 workspace and its basic tools.

Part 1: Let’s Design our Calendar (20 mins)

Follow these steps to design your calendar:

  1. Open Fusion360 and create a new project called “myDynamicCalendar”.
  2. Use the sketch tool to create a rectangle with rounded corners, set dimensions to X by X.
  3. Add another rectangle for the slider. Set its position and dimensions as required.
  4. Next, add holes to the main rectangle for finger joints, dimension the rectangles to fit the joints.
  5. Use the mirror tool to replicate your holes to save time.
  6. Use linear patterning for the small rectangles, and add the proper dimensions and spacing.
  7. Optional: Import a .dxf file for adding text or additional design elements.

Checkoff 1

Ask your instructor for a check-off after completing the design steps above.

Part 2: Laser Cutting (30 mins)

Prepare the material (wood) and load your design into the laser cutter. Set appropriate cutting speed and power settings based on the material. Once the cutting is complete, remove the pieces and proceed to post-processing.

Checkoff 2

Demonstrate the laser-cut pieces to your instructor before proceeding to assembly.

Part 3: Assemble the Calendar and Sand the Edges (30 mins)

Use glue to assemble the laser-cut pieces. Sand the edges for a smooth finish. Optionally, use spray paint to add color and mask certain areas for a clean, finished look.

Checkoff 3

Show the assembled calendar to your instructor for a final check-off.

Part 4: Remixing the Design (30 mins)

Add your own creative touch to the design. You can modify the shape, add patterns, or use different post-processing techniques. Showcase your personalized design to your instructor.

Checkoff 4

Submit your remix design for feedback from your instructor.

Part 5: Exporting as DXF (3 mins)

Once your design is complete, right-click on the sketch and export it as a DXF file. Remove any unnecessary construction lines before exporting.

Checkoff 5

Ensure the DXF file is ready for use and checked by your instructor.

Submission Guidelines

At the end of the lab, submit the following:

  • Your final Fusion360 design file (DXF format)
  • Pictures of your fabricated calendar
  • A brief report outlining the design process and any challenges encountered
